How to quickly travel to the stars without defying the laws of physics

tl;dr Digitalized, pausable humans crewing a self-repairing star ship travelling at a space shuttle's speed can visit thousands of stars within a lifetime.

The second ingredient is self-repairing interstellar star ships that can replenish their resources using asteroids and solar energy. They would have to carry sufficient energy to remain functional (although potentially in hibernation) while travelling between solar systems.

You live to see these star ships

Let's - for the beauty of the idea - assume that above requirements will become possible within only one or two centuries (It's not unimaginable with today's tech). Time is Relative

With today's propulsion technology it would take the star ship thousands of years. The star ship's speed doesn't really matter because

  • It's crew sleeps/pauses during travel between stars
  • The ship is self-repairing

How does it feel?

  • The star ship is populated by a crew of said digital people.
  • While en route the people are paused.
  • From the crew's perspective no time passes between destinations.
  • The crew can check out thousands of suns, planets, moons, nebula and go where no one has gone before.
  • The downside is that contact with earth is probably futile because earth develops (for the better or worse) while these star ships travel the galaxy.
